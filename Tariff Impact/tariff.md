TARIFF_RATES (CA: general 0.25, energy 0.10, exempt 0.65; MX: general 0.25, exempt 0.49).

Most goods from Canada/Mexico face a 25% tariff. Canadian energy products (and some categories like potash/critical minerals) receive a lower rate of 10%. These are policy-set baseline rates, not data-estimated values.

CBP has publicly stated that goods meeting USMCA preferential rules of origin may be exempt from additional tariffs.

Approximately 48.9% of imports from Mexico qualify for USMCA exemption (as reported in March 2025).

`usmca_exempt_share` is a scenario parameter capturing the share of imports that can avoid the additional tariff via USMCA qualification or exemptions; we report sensitivity ranges using publicly reported claim/qualification rates.
